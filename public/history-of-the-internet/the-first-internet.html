World War 2 Early Protocols First Internet Early World Wide Web

Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn, during 1973, designed the TCP and later with Yogen Dalal and Carl Sunshine in 1971 published it. Most consider them as the inventors of the Internet.

TELENET: introduced in 1974, first internet service provider

Bob Metcalfe: in 1973, he developed the idea of the Ethernet

TCP then split into the TCP/IP to support real-time traffic. Danny Cohen, David Reed and John Shock created it.

Paul Mockapetris and Jon Postel in 1984 introduced the domain name system with the help of the DNS.
